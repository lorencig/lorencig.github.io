1. What is Magnetic Particle Imaging (MPI)?

Magnetic Particle Imaging (MPI) is an emerging tomographic technique free of radiation that directly maps superparamagnetic iron oxide nanoparticles with exceptional sensitivity and zero background signal for diverse biomedical applications [1, 2, 3]. However, its broader translation is currently hindered by a lack of standardized data analysis protocols. This deficiency introduces significant variations dependent on the user and the scanner in image reconstruction and tracer quantification across different laboratories [4, 5].

While MPI is inherently a volumetric modality, 2D images, which capture the spatial concentration of tracers as either planar projections or individual slices of a cross section, remain a crucial and rapid tool for the evaluation of image reconstruction methods and preclinical quantification [1, 6].

3. The Quantification Bottleneck

Despite its inherent sensitivity, translation is bottlenecked by the lack of standard data analysis. A fixed pipeline rarely performs optimally across varied datasets. Specifically, user-defined Regions of Interest (ROIs) introduce severe variance.

Fig. 2: The exact same spatial concentration yields highly divergent iron masses based solely on the boundary of the drawn ROI.

4. Adaptive Logic & Synergistic Benchmarking

To overcome the quantification bottleneck, we developed a multi-stage Adaptive Pipeline designed to navigate the complex analytical space of Magnetic Particle Imaging (MPI). Rather than testing methods in isolation, we integrated them into a synergistic workflow consisting of three core pillars: Detection, Filtering, and ROI Masking.

Adaptive Intelligence: The framework uses a "Smart Selector" that analyzes global image statistics (e.g., Entropy, SNR) and local parameters (e.g., Eccentricity, FWHM) to dynamically choose the optimal algorithm configuration for each specific dataset.

Integrated Algorithm Library: We benchmarked the combinatorial synergy of 40 Classical methods (e.g., Local Maxima, Radial Profiling) and 10 Machine Learning models (e.g., GMM, DBSCAN clustering).

Massive Scale: By evaluating these hybrid configurations across diverse SPION formulations in terms of shape, size, composition, concentration, in varying mediums, volumes, and sample holders, the framework executed over 1.1 million iterative tests to define the optimal performance frontier.

Fig. 3: R² landscape across >10,000 unique pipeline configurations. No single method dominates across all conditions.

6. Results and Conclusions

Fig. 5 (B): MPI signal versus ICP-OES iron quantification under the pipeline configuration chosen by the adaptive software; least-squares fit yields R² = 0.997, corresponding to >99.7% linear agreement with the ICP-OES reference on average.

  • First open-source standardised framework for 2D MPI absolute iron quantification
  • Exhaustive benchmarking of >1.1 M pipeline iterations across diverse SPION conditions
  • Adaptive algorithm outperforms any fixed-pipeline approach, no universal method exists
  • Manual configuration mode ensures strict cross-laboratory reproducibility
  • In-house developed peak detection outperforms the other mathematical and ML methods tested.
  • Iron quantification linearity versus ICP-OES is above 99%.

7. Future Directions

  • Translational Validation: Extending to ex vivo and in vivo scenarios characterizing highly irregular spatial iron distributions.
  • Signal Deconvolution: Applying mathematical deconvolution models to separate overlapping Point Spread Functions (PSFs) characteristic of dense in vivo accumulation.
  • 3D Expansion: Adapting the algorithms natively for volumetric datasets.
